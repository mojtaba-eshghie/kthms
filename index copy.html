Available Topics

Visualization of smart contract transactions

Smart contract are programs that run on top of the blockchain, and execute rules based on certain conditions being satisfied. They can interact in the form of calling, getting called, and sending transactions each other. Understanding interactions between smart contracts requires a platform to monitor and show the transactions sent and received to the smart contracts. There is currently no open-source platform/browser addon/IDE extension that can help a developer with this task. There is a great opportunity to develop it as part of Eclipse Trace Compass: https://github.com/tracecompass/tracecompass

Structural Analysis of Proof of Stake (PoS) Blockchain Platforms

Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchain platforms work based on agreeing on a global state relying on committee decision. The committee is elected based on their stakes involved. Since these PoS blockchains only require a small fraction of energy of Proof-of-Work (PoW) blockchains, they are more sustainable. Therefore, more desirable to operate. As you may know, Ethereum is currently moving to PoS. This entails a huge effort in deploying networks that support the main Ethereum network in performing its consensus. However, Ethereum is not the only blockchain network that is moving towards PoS. In fact, there are other PoS platforms that are operational and might even have a brighter future! In this research project we will investigate the structural differences between these platforms, and do a comprehensive review and analysis of their limitations as well as benefits of design choices.

Analysis and Comparison of Smart Contract Languages

Smart contracts are multi-party agreements that execute on top of a blockchain platform based on satisfaction of certain conditions. We need programming languages that not only run on top of blockchain but also provide specific capabilities of DApp development. Currently, there are a few of these languages such as Solidity, Reach, Yul, Teal, PyTeal. There is no research that focuses on analyzing and comparing these languages on different levels: syntactic, semantic, execution environment, and interoperability. We intend to do the mentioned analysis.
